Angelfish are omnivores (their diet is based both on the plants and animals). Marine angelfish like to eat sponges, algae, jellyfish and small fish. Freshwater angelfish are more carnivorous in nature. ... They become territorial and aggressive toward other fish when they reach adulthood.

Blushing Angelfish

The beautifully colored Blushing Angel is sure to make a unique addition to your aquarium. The Blushing Angelfish has gold and black markings on its head. The young specimens will have a red hue beneath the eyes that fades with age. It prefers a well-planted tank of at least 30 gallons with soft, slightly acidic water.

Koi Angelfish

The Koi Angelfish is a strain of angelfish that has been bred for it's mottled black and white coloration. ... These are beautiful angelfish, and like Koi, the coloration of each fish will be different. It prefers a well-planted aquarium of at least 30 gallons with soft, slightly acidic water.

Platinum Blue Angelfish

Angels are a beautiful, mystical addition to any aquatic habitat. This particular freshwater angelfish is quite rare, and because of its shiny, almost metallic scales, it is often referred to as “Platinum” or “Platinum Blue”. A beautiful enhancement to any tank, these stunning angels reflect light off their scales and flowing fins, making them look extremely magnificent.
